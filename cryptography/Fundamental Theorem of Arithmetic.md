# Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic
Any positive integer $n \geq 2$ has a unique prime factorization. In other words,
$$
n = p_{1}^{e_{1}} \dots p_{r}^{e_{r}}
$$
where $p_{i}$ are primes, and $e_{i}$ are positive integers. The expression is unique up to index reordering.
