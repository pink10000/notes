# Central Limit Theorem 
Let $Y_{1}, Y_{2}, \dots, Y_{N}$ be independent, identically distributed random variables with [[Expectation#Definition|mean]] $\mu$ and [[Variance|variance]] $\sigma^{2}$. Then 
$$
\frac{
\left( \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^{N} Y_{N} \right) - \mu  
}{
\sigma/\sqrt{N}
} \to 
Z \sim N(0, 1)
$$
or the [[Normal|standard normal]].
